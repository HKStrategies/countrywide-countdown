FAQS

About the agents of change
  • So what is Agents of Change?
    We know how great it is to work in the property industry but our public perception does us no favours. Countrywide are launching a company wide initiative to challenge these perceptions and change our industry forever. We’re looking for a team of agents from across our business to lead this mission. The programme is open to all employees, not just management.
  • Will I be responsible for a certain number of Agents?
    You are asked to support the Change Agents in your team as described above.
  • How do I get selected to be an Agent of Change?
    You need to apply on the dedicated Agents of Change microsite and your application will then be reviewed by the leadership team and the internal communications and engagement team. We’re looking for people who are enthusiastic and want to help make Countrywide a great place to work, so bear that in mind when applying!
  • How much time will being an Agent of Change take up?
    The training day will consist of an overnight stay the night before the day and then a full day out of the office. After that, we anticipate that you will spend up to 1 hour a day on your mission. Your manager will support you in the time you need and you will receive a new mission every quarter.
  • Is this just an opportunity for management level or is it open to anyone?
    We all have a part to play in making Countrywide a great place to work so Agents of Change is open to everyone! It most definitely is not just for management – we want people at all levels and with a wide range of experience and expertise to join the mission and become our Agents of Change.
  • Do I have to apply?
    You don’t have to apply but if you want to make a difference to life at Countrywide and help us share best practice so we all really start living the values then why wouldn’t you?!
  • If I am a successful applicant when do I become an Agent of Change?
    We’ll be recruiting Change Agents between 28th October and 11th November. All applicants will be emailed on or around 18th November to let them know whether or not they have been successful.
  • Will I be rewarded for being an Agent of Change?
    There won’t be a reward as such, but you will get the credibility that comes with being a Change Agent and the recognition that you’re striving to help bring us all together as One Countrywide. You’ll also develop your skillset at the training day, so it really is a great opportunity to learn and help shape change at Countrywide.
  • What happens to possible commissions and targets I may miss as a result of being an Agent of Change?
    Being a Change Agent is voluntary and you will have the full support of your manager to enable you to carry out your mission without it affecting your core role.
  • Do I get gadgets, like James Bond?
    While we’d love to act like Q and give you loads of cool gadgets, all we can offer is the credibility that comes with being an Agent of Change.
